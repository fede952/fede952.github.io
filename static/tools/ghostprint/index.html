Why this is a big deal

Cookies are easy to block and delete. A fingerprint is not: it's built from the way your specific device, GPU, fonts and settings combine, so it stays the same across sites — and survives incognito mode, cleared cookies and most "private" browsing. Ad-tech and anti-fraud networks use exactly this to follow you.

The score above is an entropy estimate: how many bits of identifying information your browser leaks, and roughly how many other browsers would share your exact combination. More bits → rarer → easier to single you out.

How to fade the ghost

  • Tor Browser — the gold standard: every user is deliberately made to look identical.
  • Firefox — set privacy.resistFingerprinting to true (about:config), or use the built-in protections.
  • Brave — ships randomized canvas/audio fingerprinting by default.
  • Disable WebGL and canvas readout, or use anti-fingerprint extensions (CanvasBlocker, etc.).
  • Prefer stock hardware/fonts and a default window size — rare setups are more identifiable, not less.
GhostPrint reads only what the standard Web APIs already expose to any page — nothing is transmitted.
Entropy figures are estimates based on public browser-fingerprinting research (EFF, AmIUnique) and your live signals.
