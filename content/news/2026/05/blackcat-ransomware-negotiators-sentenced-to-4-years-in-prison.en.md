Two former incident response employees from Sygnia and DigitalMint received four-year prison sentences for aiding BlackCat ransomware attacks against US companies.

{{< cyber-report severity="High" source="BleepingComputer" target="US companies" >}}

Two former employees of cybersecurity incident response firms Sygnia and DigitalMint have been sentenced to four years in prison each for their roles in BlackCat (ALPHV) ransomware attacks targeting US organizations. The individuals acted as negotiators, facilitating ransom payments and communications between victims and the threat actors.

The sentencing highlights the legal risks faced by professionals who cross the line from legitimate incident response to active collaboration with ransomware gangs. The case underscores that even indirect participation in ransomware operations, such as negotiating payments on behalf of attackers, can lead to severe criminal penalties.

BlackCat/ALPHV is a ransomware-as-a-service operation that has targeted numerous sectors globally. This prosecution signals increased scrutiny of the ransomware ecosystem, including enablers and intermediaries, as part of broader efforts to disrupt cybercriminal supply chains.
