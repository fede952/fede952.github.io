Ryan Goldberg and Kevin Martin received four-year prison sentences for deploying BlackCat ransomware against U.S. victims between April and December 2023.

{{< cyber-report severity="High" source="The Hacker News" target="U.S. organizations" >}}

The U.S. Department of Justice announced on Thursday that two cybersecurity professionals, Ryan Goldberg and Kevin Martin, have been sentenced to four years in prison for their involvement in BlackCat ransomware attacks. The duo deployed the ransomware against multiple victims across the United States between April and December 2023.

{{< ad-banner >}}

Goldberg, 40, of Georgia, and Martin, 36, of Texas, were accused of facilitating the attacks, which targeted various organizations. The sentencing underscores the legal consequences for cybersecurity professionals who misuse their skills for malicious purposes.

The BlackCat ransomware group, also known as ALPHV, has been active since late 2021, targeting critical infrastructure and demanding large ransoms. This case highlights the ongoing threat posed by ransomware and the importance of insider threat detection.
