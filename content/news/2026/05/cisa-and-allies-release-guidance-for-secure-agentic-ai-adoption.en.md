New international guidance from CISA and partners outlines security risks and best practices for designing, deploying, and operating agentic AI systems safely.

{{< cyber-report severity="Info" source="CISA" target="Organizations adopting agentic AI" >}}

CISA, in collaboration with the Australian Signals Directorate’s Australian Cyber Security Centre (ASD’s ACSC) and other international and U.S. partners, has released a guide titled 'Careful Adoption of Agentic AI Services.' The document addresses key security challenges and risks associated with agentic artificial intelligence systems, which can autonomously plan and execute actions to achieve goals.

{{< ad-banner >}}

The guidance provides actionable steps for designing, deploying, and operating agentic AI systems safely. It helps organizations align AI risk management with existing cybersecurity frameworks and strengthen oversight as adoption of these systems grows. The release emphasizes the need for robust security controls, continuous monitoring, and clear governance structures.

CISA is soliciting feedback on the guide through its product survey, indicating that the guidance may evolve based on community input. This collaborative approach reflects the rapidly changing landscape of AI security and the importance of international cooperation in addressing emerging threats.
