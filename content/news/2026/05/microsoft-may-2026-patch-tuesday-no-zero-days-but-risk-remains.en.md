For the first time since June 2024, Microsoft's May 2026 Patch Tuesday includes no actively exploited zero-days. However, the absence of exploits does not mean absence of risk, as MDASH AI debuts.

{{< cyber-report severity="Medium" source="Cybersecurity360" target="Microsoft Windows and related products" >}}

Microsoft's May 2026 Patch Tuesday marks a notable milestone: no zero-day vulnerabilities were reported as actively exploited, the first such occurrence since June 2024. While this may seem reassuring, security experts caution that the absence of known exploits does not equate to a risk-free environment. Organizations should still prioritize patching, as unpatched vulnerabilities remain attractive targets for threat actors.

{{< ad-banner >}}

This month also introduces MDASH, Microsoft's new AI-driven system for automated vulnerability discovery. MDASH aims to accelerate the identification of security flaws, potentially reducing the window between discovery and patch release. However, the effectiveness and potential false positives of this system remain to be seen.

Despite the lack of zero-days, the cumulative risk from unpatched systems and the evolving threat landscape demands continued vigilance. Security teams should treat this Patch Tuesday as an opportunity to catch up on backlog updates and strengthen their overall security posture.
