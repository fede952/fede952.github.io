Belarus-aligned threat actor Ghostwriter uses Prometheus-themed phishing emails to target Ukrainian government entities, deploying malware via compromised accounts.

{{< cyber-report severity="High" source="The Hacker News" target="Ukrainian government entities" >}}

The Belarus-aligned threat actor known as Ghostwriter (aka UAC-0057 and UNC1151) has been observed targeting government organizations in Ukraine with phishing emails that leverage lures related to Prometheus, a Ukrainian online learning platform. The Computer Emergency Response Team of Ukraine (CERT-UA) reported that the attacks involve sending malicious emails from compromised accounts to government entities.

{{< ad-banner >}}

The phishing emails are designed to deliver Prometheus-themed malware, likely as an initial access vector for espionage or disruption. Ghostwriter has historically been associated with information operations and cyber espionage aligned with Belarusian interests, and this campaign continues their focus on Ukrainian targets amid the ongoing conflict.

Organizations in Ukraine, particularly government agencies, should be vigilant for phishing emails referencing Prometheus or other educational platforms. CERT-UA recommends verifying sender identities and avoiding clicking on links or opening attachments from untrusted sources. Implementing multi-factor authentication and monitoring for anomalous account activity can help mitigate the risk of compromise.

This campaign underscores the importance of email security controls, especially for government entities in conflict zones. SOC analysts should prioritize monitoring for phishing lures referencing local platforms like Prometheus, and DevSecOps teams should enforce strict email authentication (DMARC, DKIM, SPF) to reduce spoofing risks. Additionally, compromised accounts used as initial vectors highlight the need for rapid incident response and credential rotation.
