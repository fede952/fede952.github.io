China-backed Silver Fox targets India and Russia with tax-themed phishing, deploying ABCDoor backdoor and ValleyRAT malware.

{{< cyber-report severity="High" source="Dark Reading" target="Organizations in India and Russia" >}}

The China-backed advanced persistent threat group known as Silver Fox has launched a new campaign using tax-themed social engineering to target organizations in India and Russia. The attacks involve over 1,600 socially engineered messages aimed at various sectors, delivering previously undocumented malware including the ABCDoor backdoor and ValleyRAT.

{{< ad-banner >}}

The ABCDoor backdoor is a new addition to Silver Fox's arsenal, designed to establish persistent access and exfiltrate data. ValleyRAT, a known remote access trojan, is also deployed in these attacks. The campaign highlights the group's continued focus on financial and governmental entities, leveraging timely tax themes to increase victim engagement.

Security researchers urge organizations in the affected regions to enhance email filtering and user awareness training, as the attacks rely heavily on social engineering. Indicators of compromise (IOCs) associated with the campaign should be monitored, and network defenses updated to detect the new backdoor and RAT.

{{< netrunner-insight >}}

SOC analysts should prioritize monitoring for tax-themed phishing emails and deploy behavioral detection rules for the ABCDoor backdoor's network signatures. DevSecOps teams must ensure endpoint detection and response (EDR) tools are tuned to identify ValleyRAT's persistence mechanisms, and consider blocking known C2 infrastructure associated with Silver Fox.
