Brazilian anti-DDoS firm allegedly enabled botnet attacks on rival ISPs; CEO claims breach and competitor sabotage.

{{< cyber-report severity="High" source="Krebs on Security" target="Brazilian ISPs" >}}

A Brazilian company specializing in DDoS protection services has been implicated in a sustained campaign of distributed denial-of-service attacks against other network operators in Brazil. According to KrebsOnSecurity, the firm's infrastructure was used to power a botnet that launched massive DDoS attacks, contradicting its stated mission of defending networks.

{{< ad-banner >}}

The company's CEO claims the malicious activity stemmed from a security breach and suggests a competitor may be responsible for tarnishing the firm's reputation. However, the incident raises serious questions about the security posture of organizations entrusted with mitigating cyber threats.

{{< netrunner-insight >}}

This incident underscores the importance of vetting third-party security vendors and monitoring for anomalous outbound traffic, even from trusted partners. SOC analysts should treat DDoS protection services as potential attack vectors and implement strict access controls and logging for any infrastructure with elevated network privileges.
