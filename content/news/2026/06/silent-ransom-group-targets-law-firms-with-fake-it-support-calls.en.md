Mandiant reports Silent Ransom Group is using social engineering to breach U.S. law firms, stealing data within hours of initial contact.

{{< cyber-report severity="High" source="BleepingComputer" target="U.S. law firms and professional services" >}}

The Silent Ransom Group extortion gang is actively targeting U.S. law firms and professional services organizations in social engineering attacks that often lead to data theft within hours of initial contact, according to a new report by cybersecurity firm Mandiant.

{{< ad-banner >}}

The attackers use fake IT support calls to gain initial access, then rapidly deploy ransomware and exfiltrate sensitive data. The speed of the attack chain suggests a well-rehearsed playbook aimed at maximizing disruption and leverage for ransom demands.

Law firms are particularly attractive targets due to the sensitive client data they hold, which can be used for extortion or sold on dark web markets. Organizations in the legal sector should review their incident response plans and educate staff on social engineering tactics.

{{< netrunner-insight >}}

SOC analysts should monitor for unusual IT support requests and verify caller identity through out-of-band channels. DevSecOps teams should implement strict access controls and data loss prevention measures to limit the blast radius of initial compromise.
