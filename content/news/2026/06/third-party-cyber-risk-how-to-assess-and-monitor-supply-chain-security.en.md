A guide to evaluating, monitoring, and contracting third-party cybersecurity in the supply chain, in line with NIS2 and DORA regulations.

{{< cyber-report severity="Info" source="Cybersecurity360" target="Supply chain third parties" >}}

Third-party cyber risk is one of the most insidious threats for organizations today. Supply chain attacks exploit trusted vendors to bypass perimeter defenses, making it critical to assess and monitor the security posture of all partners.

{{< ad-banner >}}

This guide outlines how to evaluate, monitor, and contractually enforce security throughout the supply chain, within the framework of the NIS2 Directive and the Digital Operational Resilience Act (DORA). It emphasizes continuous risk assessment and contractual safeguards.

Organizations must adopt a proactive approach to third-party risk management, including regular audits, security scorecards, and incident response coordination. The regulatory landscape is pushing for greater accountability across the entire vendor ecosystem.

{{< netrunner-insight >}}

For SOC analysts and DevSecOps engineers, this means integrating third-party risk into your threat intelligence feeds and CI/CD pipelines. Automate vendor security assessments and ensure contracts include breach notification clauses. Don't just trust your vendors—verify their security posture continuously.
