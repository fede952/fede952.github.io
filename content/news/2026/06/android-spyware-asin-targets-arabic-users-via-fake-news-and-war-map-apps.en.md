ESET uncovers Asin spyware targeting Arabic-speaking users through fake utility, war update, and government news apps since early 2025.

{{< cyber-report severity="High" source="The Hacker News" target="Arabic-speaking Android users" >}}

ESET has identified a new Android spyware campaign dubbed Asin that specifically targets Arabic-speaking users. The malware has been spreading since early 2025 through multiple attack waves, each using distinct websites that mimic legitimate utilities, war-related updates, and a government news source at govlens[.]net.

{{< ad-banner >}}

The spyware is distributed via fake apps posing as news readers, PDF viewers, and war map applications. Once installed, Asin can steal sensitive data including contacts, messages, call logs, and device information. The campaign demonstrates a focused effort to compromise users in Arabic-speaking regions through socially engineered lures.

ESET's findings highlight the continued evolution of mobile spyware targeting specific linguistic and geopolitical groups. The use of multiple themed websites suggests a well-resourced operation capable of adapting its lures to current events and regional interests.

{{< netrunner-insight >}}

For SOC analysts, monitor network traffic to domains like govlens[.]net and similar lookalikes. DevSecOps teams should enforce sideloading restrictions and educate users about the risks of installing apps from untrusted sources, especially those themed around regional news or conflict updates.
