CISA urges Fortinet customers to secure FortiGate appliances against ongoing attacks by Russian-speaking threat actors, with over 86,000 devices compromised in the FortiBleed campaign.

{{< cyber-report severity="High" source="The Hacker News" target="Fortinet FortiGate appliances" >}}

The U.S. Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency (CISA) has issued an urgent warning to Fortinet customers following a large-scale campaign targeting FortiGate appliances. The operation, dubbed FortiBleed, has compromised 86,644 internet-accessible devices and is attributed to Russian-speaking threat actors.

{{< ad-banner >}}

CISA's advisory urges organizations to take immediate steps to secure their FortiGate appliances, though specific vulnerabilities or exploitation methods have not been disclosed. The scale of the compromise suggests a coordinated and automated scanning and exploitation effort, likely leveraging known weaknesses or misconfigurations.

Fortinet customers are advised to review their device configurations, apply available patches, and monitor for signs of unauthorized access. The campaign underscores the persistent threat posed by state-aligned groups targeting network edge devices, which serve as critical chokepoints for enterprise security.
