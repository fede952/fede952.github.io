HD Moore, creator of Metasploit, argues that zero-days are inevitable; the real defense is controlling what attackers can reach after a breach.

{{< cyber-report severity="Info" source="The Hacker News" target="network defenders" >}}

In a recent webinar, HD Moore, the creator of Metasploit, challenged the traditional patch-everything mindset, arguing that zero-day exploits are inevitable and AI is accelerating exploit development. He emphasized that organizations cannot control which vulnerabilities are discovered, but they can control the network segmentation and access controls that limit an attacker's lateral movement.

{{< ad-banner >}}

Moore's core thesis is that most teams have the wrong network shape, allowing a single compromise to cascade into a full breach. He advocates for assuming breach and designing networks to contain attackers, reducing the blast radius even when a zero-day is used.

The webinar, titled 'Beyond the Zero-Day: See Your Network Like an Attacker,' is aimed at helping security teams shift from reactive patching to proactive network hardening, using attacker perspectives to identify and close hidden pathways.

{{< netrunner-insight >}}

Stop betting your organization on winning the patch race. Instead, map your network's attack surface as an attacker would, then ruthlessly segment and restrict lateral movement. The shape of your network is your strongest control—make it work for you, not against you.
