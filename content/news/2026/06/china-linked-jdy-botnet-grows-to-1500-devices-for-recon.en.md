JDY botnet resurges with over 1,500 SOHO and IoT devices, used by China-nexus actors for large-scale reconnaissance and service mapping.

{{< cyber-report severity="High" source="The Hacker News" target="SOHO and IoT devices" >}}

Cybersecurity researchers have warned of a resurgence and expansion of JDY, a covert botnet linked to China-nexus state-sponsored threat actors. The botnet now comprises over 1,500 small office and home office (SOHO) and IoT devices, operating as a centrally controlled, high-performance scanner.

{{< ad-banner >}}

JDY is used to discover, fingerprint, and continuously map exposed services at scale, enabling threat actors to identify potential targets for further exploitation. The expansion highlights the ongoing risk posed by compromised edge devices in global networks.

Organizations should audit their SOHO and IoT device inventories, ensure firmware is up to date, and monitor for unusual scanning activity originating from known or suspicious IP ranges associated with the botnet.

{{< netrunner-insight >}}

SOC analysts should monitor for scanning patterns from large IP ranges targeting common service ports, as JDY's scale makes it noisy. DevSecOps teams must enforce strict network segmentation for IoT and SOHO devices to limit lateral movement if compromised.
