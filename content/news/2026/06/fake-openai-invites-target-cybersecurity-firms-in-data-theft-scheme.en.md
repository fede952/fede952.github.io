Threat actors impersonate legitimate companies via OpenAI tenants to trick employees into sharing sensitive data in chats and projects.

{{< cyber-report severity="Medium" source="BleepingComputer" target="cybersecurity firms" >}}

Threat actors are creating fraudulent OpenAI tenants that impersonate legitimate companies, then sending organization invites to employees of cybersecurity firms. The goal appears to be tricking targets into submitting sensitive company information within chats and projects hosted on the fake tenants.

{{< ad-banner >}}

This social engineering campaign leverages the trust associated with OpenAI's collaboration platform. By mimicking a known organization, attackers lower the victim's guard, increasing the likelihood of data exposure.

The campaign specifically targets cybersecurity professionals, potentially aiming to steal threat intelligence, client data, or internal security procedures. No technical exploitation of OpenAI's platform has been reported; the attack relies purely on deception.

{{< netrunner-insight >}}

SOC analysts should educate users to verify any unexpected organization invites, especially those requesting sensitive input. DevSecOps teams should consider implementing strict policies for external collaboration platforms and monitoring for anomalous tenant creation or data uploads.
