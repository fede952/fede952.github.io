CERT-AGID reports over 650 phishing and spam incidents via PEC (Certified Email) in 2026, involving compromised accounts and malicious registrations.

{{< cyber-report severity="Medium" source="CERT-AgID" target="Italian PEC email users" >}}

CERT-AGID has detected a sustained wave of phishing and spam campaigns targeting Italian Certified Electronic Mail (PEC) users. The agency reports handling over 650 malicious events in 2026, with threat actors leveraging compromised legitimate PEC accounts or registering new accounts for illicit purposes.

{{< ad-banner >}}

The attacks exploit the trust associated with PEC, which is legally recognized for official communications in Italy. Malicious emails are designed to trick recipients into revealing credentials or sensitive information, often impersonating government agencies or financial institutions.

CERT-AGID reassures citizens and operators that countermeasures are in place, including continuous monitoring and coordination with email service providers. Users are advised to verify unexpected PEC messages through alternative channels and report suspicious emails.

{{< netrunner-insight >}}

For SOC analysts: prioritize monitoring of PEC traffic anomalies, especially sudden spikes in outbound emails from legitimate accounts. DevSecOps teams should enforce multi-factor authentication and anomaly detection on email gateways to mitigate account compromise risks.
