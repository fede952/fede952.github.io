Google Mandiant and GTIG attribute a financially motivated campaign to UNC3753, targeting professional, legal, and financial services in the U.S. from Jan to May 2026.

{{< cyber-report severity="High" source="The Hacker News" target="U.S. professional, legal, and financial services" >}}

Cybersecurity researchers have disclosed details of a financially motivated data theft extortion campaign that has targeted dozens of organizations across professional, legal, and financial services in the U.S. between January and May 2026. The activity has been attributed by Google Mandiant and Google Threat Intelligence Group (GTIG) to a threat actor dubbed UNC3753.

{{< ad-banner >}}

The campaign reportedly involves vishing (voice phishing) and physical intrusions to gain initial access and exfiltrate sensitive data. The attackers then leverage the stolen data for extortion purposes, threatening to release it unless a ransom is paid.

Organizations in the targeted sectors are advised to enhance their security awareness training, implement multi-factor authentication, and monitor for unusual physical access attempts. The use of vishing highlights the need for strict verification protocols for phone-based requests.

{{< netrunner-insight >}}

SOC analysts should monitor for vishing attempts targeting employees and ensure robust physical security controls are in place. DevSecOps teams should review access logs for anomalies and enforce least-privilege principles to limit data exposure in case of a breach.
