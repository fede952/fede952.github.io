CISA, FBI, NSA, and other agencies warn of malicious cyber activity targeting internet-exposed automatic tank gauge systems in critical infrastructure sectors.

{{< cyber-report severity="High" source="CISA" target="Automatic tank gauge (ATG) systems" >}}

The Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency (CISA), along with the FBI, NSA, DOE, EPA, TSA, DOT, and USDA, has issued a joint fact sheet urging owners and operators of automatic tank gauge (ATG) systems to harden their systems against malicious cyber activity. ATG systems are widely used across the Energy, Chemical, Food and Agriculture, and Transportation Systems Sectors for remote monitoring of storage tank parameters such as fuel levels, temperature, and leak detection.

{{< ad-banner >}}

The authoring organizations report that cyber threat actors have been observed compromising internet-exposed ATG systems and executing commands to modify their operation. While the U.S. government has not attributed the activity to a specific nation-state or group, the advisory outlines probable tactics, techniques, and procedures (TTPs) used in these attacks.

To mitigate risk, the agencies recommend removing ATG systems from the internet to reduce public exposure and enforcing strong password policies. The advisory emphasizes that securing these operational technology assets is critical to preventing disruption to fuel supply chains and other essential services.
