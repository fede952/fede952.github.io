International law enforcement dismantles SocGholish botnet infrastructure, cleaning nearly 15,000 WordPress sites and seizing over 100 servers linked to Russian cybercrime group Evil Corp.

{{< cyber-report severity="High" source="BleepingComputer" target="WordPress websites" >}}

International law enforcement agencies have successfully cleaned nearly 15,000 WordPress websites infected with the SocGholish malware, which is associated with the Russian cybercrime group Evil Corp. The operation also involved the takedown of over 100 servers that supported the botnet infrastructure.

{{< ad-banner >}}

SocGholish, also known as FakeUpdates, is a JavaScript-based dropper that typically spreads through compromised websites, tricking users into downloading malicious payloads. The malware has been used to deliver ransomware and other threats, making it a significant concern for website administrators and security teams.

The coordinated effort highlights the ongoing collaboration between global law enforcement to disrupt cybercriminal operations. While the immediate threat has been reduced, organizations are advised to ensure their WordPress installations are patched and monitored for signs of compromise.

{{< netrunner-insight >}}

This takedown is a win, but don't let your guard down. Ensure your WordPress sites are hardened with regular updates, strong credentials, and file integrity monitoring. For SOC analysts, watch for unusual JavaScript downloads or outbound connections to known malicious IPs—these are classic SocGholish indicators.
