The National Association of Insurance Commissioners says ShinyHunters exploited an Oracle PeopleSoft zero-day to steal public data, logs, and config files.

{{< cyber-report severity="Medium" source="BleepingComputer" target="Oracle PeopleSoft server" >}}

The National Association of Insurance Commissioners (NAIC) has confirmed that the ShinyHunters extortion group breached its systems by exploiting a zero-day vulnerability in an Oracle PeopleSoft server. The attackers stole only publicly available data, outdated logs, and configuration files, according to the NAIC's statement.

{{< ad-banner >}}

The breach was attributed to the ShinyHunters group, which is known for targeting corporate databases and extorting victims. The NAIC emphasized that no sensitive consumer or insurer data was compromised, as the stolen information was already public or non-critical.

The incident highlights the risks posed by unpatched vulnerabilities in legacy enterprise software like Oracle PeopleSoft. Organizations using similar systems are advised to prioritize patching and monitor for indicators of compromise associated with ShinyHunters' tactics.

{{< netrunner-insight >}}

SOC teams should monitor for exploitation of Oracle PeopleSoft zero-days, as ShinyHunters actively targets these systems. Ensure that PeopleSoft servers are isolated and logs are reviewed for unusual access patterns. Even 'public data' breaches can lead to reputational damage and follow-on attacks.
