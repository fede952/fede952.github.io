Dutch-led law enforcement operation disrupts SocGholish malware infrastructure and cleans nearly 15,000 infected WordPress websites, cutting off cybercriminal access.

{{< cyber-report severity="High" source="The Hacker News" target="WordPress websites" >}}

Law enforcement agencies from the Netherlands, Canada, Germany, and the United States have jointly disrupted the infrastructure behind SocGholish, a malware distribution platform commonly used to deliver ransomware and other payloads. The operation, dubbed Operation Endgame, resulted in the cleanup of 14,971 compromised WordPress websites that were being used as part of the malicious network.

{{< ad-banner >}}

According to Maikel Rollman of the Netherlands National High Tech Crime Unit, the action deprives cybercriminals of access to infected systems. SocGholish has historically been associated with initial access brokers and ransomware groups, leveraging compromised websites to distribute fake browser updates that lead to further compromise.

The takedown represents a significant blow to the supply chain of cybercrime, as SocGholish infrastructure has been a persistent threat for years. The cleanup of thousands of WordPress sites reduces the attack surface available for future campaigns, though similar threats may emerge in response.

For SOC analysts, this operation underscores the importance of monitoring outbound connections from internal hosts to known malicious domains. DevSecOps teams should prioritize regular scanning and patching of WordPress installations, as outdated plugins and themes remain a primary vector for compromise.
