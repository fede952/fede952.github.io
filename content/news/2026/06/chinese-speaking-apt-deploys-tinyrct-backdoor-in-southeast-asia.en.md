A Chinese-speaking APT group targets government and energy sectors in Southeast Asia with a new custom backdoor called TinyRCT, attributed to CL-STA-1062.

{{< cyber-report severity="High" source="The Hacker News" target="Government and energy sectors in Southeast Asia" >}}

A Chinese-speaking advanced persistent threat (APT) actor has been linked to a new custom backdoor called TinyRCT in cyber attacks targeting government entities and critical infrastructure in Southeast Asia. The campaign specifically focuses on state-owned enterprises in the energy and government sectors.

{{< ad-banner >}}

The activity has been attributed to a threat actor designated CL-STA-1062 by Palo Alto Networks. The backdoor TinyRCT is a custom tool used to maintain persistence and exfiltrate data from compromised networks.

Organizations in the region, particularly those in energy and government, should review their security posture for indicators of compromise related to TinyRCT and CL-STA-1062. Network segmentation and endpoint detection are critical to mitigating this threat.

{{< netrunner-insight >}}

SOC analysts should monitor for unusual outbound connections and process creation patterns indicative of TinyRCT. DevSecOps teams must ensure that critical infrastructure systems are isolated and that least-privilege principles are enforced to limit lateral movement.
