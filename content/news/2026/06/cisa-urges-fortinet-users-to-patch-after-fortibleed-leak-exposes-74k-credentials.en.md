CISA warns Fortinet customers to secure devices after nearly 74,000 firewall and VPN credentials were leaked in the FortiBleed incident.

{{< cyber-report severity="High" source="BleepingComputer" target="Fortinet firewall and VPN devices" >}}

The U.S. Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency (CISA) has issued an urgent warning to Fortinet customers following the exposure of nearly 74,000 firewall and VPN credentials in a data leak dubbed 'FortiBleed.' The leaked credentials could allow attackers to gain unauthorized access to affected devices, potentially leading to network compromise and data breaches.

{{< ad-banner >}}

Fortinet has not yet confirmed a specific CVE associated with this leak, but CISA's advisory emphasizes the need for immediate action. Organizations are advised to review their Fortinet device configurations, rotate all credentials, and apply any available security updates to mitigate the risk.

The FortiBleed incident highlights the ongoing threat of credential leaks targeting network edge devices. Security teams should prioritize monitoring for unauthorized access attempts and implement multi-factor authentication (MFA) to reduce the impact of such exposures.
