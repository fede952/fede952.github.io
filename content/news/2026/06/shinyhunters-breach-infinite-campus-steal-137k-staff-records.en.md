The attack, which occurred in March, involved a Salesforce data theft that compromised over 137,000 school staff accounts, exposing personal information.

The stolen data includes names, email addresses, and other personally identifiable information (PII) of school staff. Infinite Campus has not yet disclosed the full scope of the breach or whether student data was affected, but the incident highlights the vulnerability of educational technology platforms to targeted extortion campaigns.

ShinyHunters is known for selling stolen data on underground forums and has previously targeted major companies. The breach underscores the need for robust security measures in cloud-based educational systems, particularly those handling sensitive staff and student information.
