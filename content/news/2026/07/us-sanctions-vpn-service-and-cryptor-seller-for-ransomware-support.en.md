OFAC designates First VPN Service and two individuals for enabling ransomware attacks, marking first sanctions against a VPN provider.

{{< cyber-report severity="High" source="The Hacker News" target="Ransomware actors and cybercriminals" >}}

The U.S. Treasury Department's OFAC has sanctioned First VPN Service (1VPNS), a VPN provider accused of offering its tools to ransomware groups, along with two individuals, including a 45-year-old Ukrainian malware cryptor seller. This marks the first time a VPN service has been sanctioned for supporting ransomware activities.

{{< ad-banner >}}

The sanctions aim to disrupt the infrastructure used by ransomware actors to obfuscate their operations and evade law enforcement. The designated entities are alleged to have provided critical services such as bulletproof hosting and malware obfuscation, enabling attacks against American targets.

This action underscores the U.S. government's increasing focus on targeting enablers of ransomware, including financial facilitators and technical service providers. The sanctions freeze any U.S.-based assets of the designated parties and prohibit transactions with them.
