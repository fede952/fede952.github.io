Attackers exploiting Fortinet firewall vulnerabilities and a Nextcloud zero-day are now collaborating with ransomware groups to monetize access.

{{< cyber-report severity="High" source="Dark Reading" target="Fortinet firewalls, Nextcloud servers" >}}

Threat actors behind the FortiBleed campaign have expanded their operations by collaborating with the Inc and Lynx ransomware gangs. After compromising thousands of Fortinet firewalls, they are now monetizing that access through ransomware deployments.

{{< ad-banner >}}

In addition to the Fortinet vulnerabilities, the attackers are also exploiting a zero-day vulnerability in Nextcloud, a popular open-source file-sharing platform. This dual-pronged approach allows them to target both perimeter devices and internal collaboration tools.

The collaboration with ransomware groups indicates a shift from initial access brokers to full-scale extortion operations. Organizations using Fortinet or Nextcloud products should prioritize patching and monitor for signs of unauthorized access.

{{< netrunner-insight >}}

This is a classic case of initial access brokers scaling up. SOC teams should hunt for unusual Fortinet VPN connections and Nextcloud login anomalies. If you have FortiGate or Nextcloud in your environment, assume compromise and run a forensic review immediately.
