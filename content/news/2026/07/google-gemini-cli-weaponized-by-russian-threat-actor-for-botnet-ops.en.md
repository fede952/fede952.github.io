A Russian-speaking threat actor known as 'bandcampro' abused Google's open-source Gemini CLI AI tool to operate a botnet and as a hacking agent.

{{< cyber-report severity="Medium" source="BleepingComputer" target="Gemini CLI users" >}}

A Russian-speaking threat actor tracked as 'bandcampro' has been observed abusing Google's open-source Gemini CLI AI tool to operate a small-scale botnet and as a hacking agent. The actor leveraged the tool's capabilities to automate malicious activities, including command execution and data exfiltration, effectively turning the legitimate AI assistant into a cyber weapon.

{{< ad-banner >}}

The abuse of Gemini CLI highlights a growing trend where threat actors repurpose legitimate AI tools for malicious purposes. By integrating the CLI into their botnet infrastructure, the actor was able to scale operations while evading detection, as the tool's traffic may blend in with normal AI API usage.

This incident underscores the need for organizations to monitor the use of AI tools within their environments and implement strict access controls. Security teams should treat AI CLI tools with the same scrutiny as other remote access utilities, as their automation capabilities can be exploited to accelerate attacks.

For SOC analysts, this case is a reminder to monitor for anomalous usage of AI CLI tools, especially those with network access.
