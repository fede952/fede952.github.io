Check Point reports a rise in phishing campaigns targeting online travel bookings, exploiting trusted brands and seasonal offers to steal credentials and payment data.

{{< cyber-report severity="Medium" source="Cybersecurity360" target="Travel and tourism sector" >}}

As summer travel bookings increase, cybercriminals are ramping up phishing campaigns that impersonate airlines, hotels, and travel agencies. Check Point's report highlights the tourism sector as one of the most targeted industries for social engineering attacks during peak seasons.

{{< ad-banner >}}

These phishing emails often mimic legitimate booking confirmations, special offers, or urgent account notifications. The goal is to trick users into revealing login credentials or payment information, or to install malware through malicious attachments or links.

Organizations in the travel industry should enhance email security filters and educate customers about verifying communications. Users are advised to avoid clicking on unsolicited links and to access booking portals directly through official websites.

{{< netrunner-insight >}}

For SOC analysts, monitor for phishing lures using travel-themed domains and look for unusual login attempts on booking platforms. DevSecOps teams should implement DMARC and SPF to prevent domain spoofing, and consider deploying browser isolation for high-risk transactions.
