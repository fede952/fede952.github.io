After four years, the Daxin kernel rootkit reappears in a Taiwan manufacturing firm, paired with a novel pre-login SYSTEM backdoor named Stupig.

{{< cyber-report severity="High" source="The Hacker News" target="Taiwan manufacturing firm" >}}

The Daxin kernel-mode rootkit, first documented by Symantec in March 2022 and attributed to a China-linked threat actor, has resurfaced after more than four years. It was found within a Taiwan manufacturing firm, indicating continued targeting of specific sectors.

{{< ad-banner >}}

Alongside Daxin, researchers discovered a previously unreported backdoor named Stupig, which operates as a pre-login SYSTEM backdoor. This combination suggests a sophisticated attack chain aimed at maintaining persistent access to compromised systems.

The reappearance of Daxin highlights the enduring threat from advanced malware families, even after extended periods of dormancy. Organizations in manufacturing and critical infrastructure should remain vigilant against such targeted attacks.
