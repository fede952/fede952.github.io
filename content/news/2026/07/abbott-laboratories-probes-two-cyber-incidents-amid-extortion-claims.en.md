Abbott Laboratories investigates two separate cybersecurity incidents: unauthorized access to legacy Exact Sciences systems and a breach of its LabCentral portal with data theft claims.

{{< cyber-report severity="High" source="BleepingComputer" target="Abbott Laboratories systems" >}}

Abbott Laboratories is investigating two separate cybersecurity incidents. The first involves unauthorized access to internal legacy Exact Sciences systems within its Cancer Diagnostics business. The second incident stems from claims that attackers breached Abbott's LabCentral portal and stole company data, with extortion demands reportedly made.

{{< ad-banner >}}

The company has confirmed the unauthorized access and is working with law enforcement and cybersecurity experts to assess the scope and impact. No further technical details have been disclosed, and it remains unclear if the two incidents are connected or if any patient or customer data was compromised.

These incidents highlight the ongoing risk to healthcare and diagnostics companies, which hold sensitive data and are frequent targets of extortion-focused cyberattacks. Organizations in this sector should review access controls, legacy system security, and incident response plans.

{{< netrunner-insight >}}

For SOC analysts, prioritize monitoring for unusual access to legacy systems and portal authentication logs. DevSecOps teams should ensure that legacy systems are segmented and that multi-factor authentication is enforced on all external-facing portals. Extortion claims suggest data exfiltration, so prepare for potential data leak investigations.
