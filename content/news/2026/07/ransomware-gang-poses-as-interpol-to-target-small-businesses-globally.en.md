A ransomware campaign uses basic social engineering, impersonating Interpol to trick small businesses across the US, Europe, and Middle East.

{{< cyber-report severity="Medium" source="Dark Reading" target="Small businesses" >}}

A ransomware campaign is leveraging social engineering by masquerading as Interpol to target small businesses across multiple regions, including the United States, Europe, and the Middle East. The attackers rely on basic manipulation tactics rather than sophisticated exploits, indicating a low barrier to entry for this threat.

{{< ad-banner >}}

The campaign's broad geographic reach suggests a coordinated effort, though specific attribution remains unclear. Small businesses are often less prepared for such targeted social engineering attacks, making them vulnerable to initial access.

Organizations should reinforce security awareness training, particularly around verifying official communications, and implement multi-factor authentication to mitigate the risk of ransomware deployment.

{{< netrunner-insight >}}

SOC analysts should monitor for phishing emails impersonating law enforcement, as this tactic preys on authority bias. DevSecOps teams can reduce impact by ensuring robust backup strategies and endpoint detection for ransomware indicators, even if initial access is gained via social engineering.
