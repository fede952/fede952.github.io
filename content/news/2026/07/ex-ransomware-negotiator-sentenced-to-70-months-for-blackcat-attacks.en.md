A former DigitalMint employee was sentenced to 70 months in prison for helping BlackCat ransomware gang target U.S. companies. The case highlights insider threats in incident response.

{{< cyber-report severity="High" source="BleepingComputer" target="U.S. companies" >}}

A former employee of cybersecurity incident response firm DigitalMint has been sentenced to 70 months in prison for his role in BlackCat (ALPHV) ransomware attacks against U.S. companies. The individual acted as a ransomware negotiator, leveraging his position to facilitate payments and communications with victims on behalf of the threat group.

{{< ad-banner >}}

The case underscores the risk of insider threats within security firms, as the employee abused his access and knowledge of incident response procedures to aid cybercriminals. BlackCat is known for its Ransomware-as-a-Service model and has targeted numerous organizations across critical infrastructure sectors.

Sentencing was handed down on July 10, 2026, following a federal investigation. The conviction serves as a warning to others who might consider using their cybersecurity expertise for malicious purposes, highlighting the legal consequences of such actions.
