A U.S. government agency paid $1 million to prevent leaked files. Kairos group may not be a ransomware gang, as no encryption was found.

{{< cyber-report severity="High" source="The Hacker News" target="U.S. government entity" >}}

A U.S. government entity paid approximately $1 million to prevent the leak of stolen files, according to a case study by Rakesh Krishnan for Ransom-ISAC. The analysis is based on a leaked negotiation chat and blockchain transaction records.

{{< ad-banner >}}

The extortion group, calling itself Kairos, received the payment but may not be a traditional ransomware gang. Krishnan found no evidence that Kairos ever encrypted any systems, suggesting the attack was purely data-theft extortion without encryption.

This case highlights the growing trend of data-theft extortion without encryption. SOC analysts should monitor for unusual data exfiltration patterns, and DevSecOps teams must prioritize data loss prevention controls over traditional ransomware defenses.
