ACN FAQs clarify Article 17 of NIS2, defining when and how agreements with cyber suppliers like SOC, MDR, and pentesting firms must be formalized and communicated.

{{< cyber-report severity="Info" source="Cybersecurity360" target="EU entities subject to NIS2" >}}

Article 17 of the NIS2 Directive, previously overshadowed by other provisions, is now receiving attention as ACN (Agenzia per la Cybersicurezza Nazionale) publishes clarifying FAQs. The article mandates formal agreements between entities and their cybersecurity service providers—including SOC, MDR, vulnerability assessment, penetration testing, red teaming, and cyber threat intelligence—when sensitive information is exchanged bidirectionally.

{{< ad-banner >}}

The FAQs specify when such agreements become necessary, which suppliers are covered, how the agreements must be structured, and the required communication procedures. This aims to ensure accountability and data protection in outsourced cybersecurity operations, particularly given the sensitive nature of the information shared.

Organizations subject to NIS2 must review their existing contracts with cyber service providers to ensure compliance with Article 17. Failure to formalize these agreements could lead to regulatory penalties and increased operational risk.

For SOC analysts and DevSecOps engineers, this means your incident response and threat intelligence workflows may soon require formalized data-sharing agreements with external partners. Start auditing your current vendor contracts now to identify gaps before regulators do. The focus on bidirectional information exchange highlights the need for clear data handling and confidentiality clauses.
