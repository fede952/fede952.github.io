A former ransomware negotiator received 70 months in prison for conspiring with BlackCat operators and targeting additional victims in 2023.

{{< cyber-report severity="Medium" source="The Hacker News" target="Ransomware victims" >}}

A 41-year-old former ransomware negotiator has been sentenced to 70 months in prison in the United States for conspiring with the now-defunct BlackCat ransomware group. The individual, identified as Martino, pleaded guilty to charges related to extorting multiple victims and collaborating with two other cybersecurity professionals to target additional victims in 2023.

{{< ad-banner >}}

Federal prosecutors described Martino's role as critical to the BlackCat operations, leveraging his negotiation expertise to facilitate ransom payments. The sentencing memorandum highlighted the severity of the crimes, emphasizing the harm caused to victims and the need for deterrence in the ransomware ecosystem.

This case underscores the legal risks for individuals who assist ransomware groups, even in roles perceived as non-technical. The conviction serves as a warning to others in the cybercriminal supply chain, from negotiators to money launderers.
